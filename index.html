About Me

Hi! My name is Caleb Heuvel-Horwitz. I am originally from New York and currently living in New York City. I graduated from Harvard University in May 2021 with a joint B.A. in Physics and Mathematics. As an undergraduate I conducted research with Susanne Yelin in her atomic, molecular, and optical physics (AMO) theory group from 2018 to 2021. I also worked with her group before coming to Harvard when I was in high school. Next, I briefly did research with John Doyle's AMO experimental group in 2021. Currently, I am working for Dmitri Basov's condensed matter group as a laboratory technician.

I am very passionate about building and creating things. Most of my undergraduate research involved writing code for dynamical simulations or building machines for experiments. I love not only learning how things work, but also applying my knowledge to real world projects.
